# A Quarter to Six

The clock reads seventeen thirty-nine,
The afternoon sun begins its slow decline.
April's twenty-fourth, two thousand and twenty-six,
The day half-dissolved in its lengthening tricks.

The golden hour whispers across the sky,
Long shadows stretch as daylight learns to sigh.
The world between the day and dusk now rests,
A hush descends on rooftops, trees, and nests.

At five-thirty-nine the teacups cool,
And office chairs tip back from desk and stool.
The last bright emails hang unread in air,
While evening settles softly everywhere.

This minute — fleeting, quiet, almost gone —
A breath before the stars decide to dawn.
Seventeen thirty-nine: a small, sweet seam,
Stitched between the waking world and dream.
