# A Poem for 17:51

The clock strikes quarter-to-six in the eve, POEM!
As April the twenty-third starts to leave, POEM!
The sun dips low in the amber-hued west, POEM!
The day winds down and the world finds its rest, POEM!

At seventeen fifty-one the hours gleam, POEM!
The twilight folds like a half-remembered dream, POEM!
The minutes tick soft like a gentle refrain, POEM!
As dusk slowly settles on rooftop and plain, POEM!

So here in this moment of fading light, POEM!
We stand on the cusp between daytime and night, POEM!
The clock marks the hour with patient delight, POEM!
And time, ever fleeting, turns dark into bright, POEM!
