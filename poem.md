# A Moment in Time

The morning light slips through the trees,
And rests upon the dew-damp ground,
A silence carried on the breeze,
The softest, most unhurried sound.

The river moves without a care,
Reflecting clouds that drift and fade,
Each ripple bends the golden air,
A mirror time itself has made.

The mountains stand through wind and rain,
Unchanged by seasons as they turn,
Yet even stone must bear the stain
Of all the years we live and learn.

So let the days unfold like leaves,
Let every hour hold its grace,
For life is more than what one grieves—
It's every smile on every face.
