# A Poem for 2:22 AM

*Written at 02:22 UTC, April 24, 2026*

---

The world holds its breath at twenty-two past two,
A hushed collaboration between the dark and dew.
The stars have dimmed their loudest, boldest shine,
And traded blazing glory for a softer, silver line.

The streets are empty rivers, still and cool,
Where lamplight spills in gold on every pool.
The clocks tick soft, conspirators of night,
Counting down the hours until the morning light.

In doorways sleep the cities, shoulder-curled,
Their dreams are doing half the work of the world.
A baker wakes, a nurse walks down a hall,
A poet writes by lamplight — that is all.

Two twenty-two: the hinge between the days,
Where yesterday still lingers in a haze,
And tomorrow holds its cards behind its back,
Patient as the stars on their familiar track.

So breathe, dear night, stretch out your quiet hours,
Let silence tend the world the way rain tends flowers.
At two twenty-two there is no need to rush —
The whole wide Earth is held inside a hush.
