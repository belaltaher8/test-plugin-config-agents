# A Poem for 18:21

The day leans into evening now,
The clock reads twenty-past eighteen,
The sun begins its long, slow bow,
And dusk arrives to paint the scene.

At six and twenty-one past noon,
The amber light adorns the sky,
The world exhales — not too soon —
As golden minutes wander by.

The coffee cups have long grown cold,
The afternoon has had its say,
Now evening gently starts to fold
The bright, unfinished hours of day.

So raise your eyes from screen and desk,
Step out and catch the fading light —
This hour, luminous andesque,
Stands poised between the day and night.
