# A Poem for 1:29 AM

The world is wrapped in velvet dark,
At half-past one, a single spark —
The clock ticks soft, the city sleeps,
While night its quiet vigil keeps.

No rush of feet, no midday roar,
Just April stars and something more:
A stillness deep that holds the air,
A silver hush beyond compare.

The moon leans low across the sky,
The hours drift like clouds gone by,
At one twenty-nine, the world stands still,
As dawn waits patient, over the hill.

So rest, dear world, in midnight's keep,
Let time itself drift off to sleep —
For twenty-nine past one, this night,
Is poetry before the light.
