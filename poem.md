# A Poem for 3:12 AM

The world is hushed, the clock reads three,
A quiet hour between night and dawn's first plea.
The stars still hold their silver post,
While dreamers linger, east to west coast.

At twelve past three the silence hums,
A lullaby before the morning comes.
The moon hangs low, a lantern pale,
Casting soft light on hill and dale.

These in-between hours belong to thought,
To half-formed dreams and lessons taught
By darkness and by solitude—
The mind in its most honest mood.

So here we are at three-twelve's grace,
Suspended in this quiet space,
Before the sun reclaims the sky
And all these tender shadows fly.
