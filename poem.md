# A Poem for 3:22 AM

*April 24, 2026 — 03:22 UTC*

---

The world is hushed at twenty-two past three,
A stolen hour that most will never see.
The stars still hold their vigil in the sky,
While half the earth is dreaming, drifting by.

The city breathes in slow, contented sighs,
No rush of feet, no urgent battle cries.
The clock ticks on — a quiet, steady beat —
Three twenty-two, and silence feels complete.

This is the hour of poets and of ghosts,
Of unread books and unattended toasts,
Of thoughts that bloom when daylight cannot drown
The softest truths the mind has handed down.

Soon April's dawn will rise and gild the east,
And all this hush will quietly be released.
But for this moment, three and twenty-two,
The night is ours — and beautiful, and true.
