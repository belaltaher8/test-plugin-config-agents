# A Quarter to Six

The afternoon is leaning low,
At seventeen past forty-two,
The golden hour begins to glow
And paints the sky in amber hue.

The workday's weight starts lifting now,
The clock reads nearly half past five,
The sun tips its departing bow
And dusk prepares its soft arrive.

The city hums a quieter tune,
The day has done its weary run,
Not quite the dusk, not quite the moon —
Just twilight chasing down the sun.

So here we stand at day's last breath,
At 5:42 in fading light,
Between the world of life and rest,
That tender door of day to night.
