# A Moment in Time

The morning light slips through the trees,
A golden thread on autumn's breeze,
The world awakens, soft and slow,
In hues of amber, rust, and glow.

A sparrow perches on the fence,
Its song both simple and immense,
It fills the air with notes so clear,
A fleeting gift for those who hear.

The river runs without a care,
It mirrors clouds and open air,
Each ripple writes and then erases
The stories told in fleeting places.

So let us pause amid the day,
Before the hours slip away,
And find, in what surrounds us still,
The quiet joy that time can't steal.
