# A Poem for 18:47

The clock strikes quarter-to-seven, golden hour aglow, POEM!
The sun dips low and paints the sky in amber, soft and slow, POEM!
At eighteen forty-seven, day and evening softly meet, POEM!
The world exhales a gentle breath along the cobblestone street, POEM!

The minutes tick like whispered dreams, the seconds start to fade, POEM!
Long shadows stretch across the lawns beneath the oak trees' shade, POEM!
The day has done its labor well, the night begins to call, POEM!
And in between these tender hours, I find the best of all, POEM!

So here I stand at forty-seven past the eighteenth chime, POEM!
A fleeting, lovely moment caught between the hands of time, POEM!
